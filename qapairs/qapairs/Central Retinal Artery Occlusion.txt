what is the difference between optic neuropathy and retinal artery occulsion?
optic neuropathy is a generic term referring to damage to the optic nerve from any cause including trauma decreased blood flow or genetic disease. retinal artery occlusion is a blockage in the artery feeding the retina which leads to damage or death of the retinal nervous tissue. a central retinal artery occlusion can cause an optic neuropathy.
